Field Observations

Something Interesting Is Happening

As we continue gathering data across our tester network, a trend has emerged that we think is worth sharing. The hives being treated with ThermaHive are looking noticeably more robust than untreated hives. Inspections are showing more young larvae, stronger population counts, and some of our testers have started reporting the same.

To give you a sense of what we're seeing: three of our ThermaHive-treated hives already have two honey supers on them. Two others are brand new colonies with queens born in May of this year that did not receive their first treatment until the end of May, and they are already showing strong, healthy development.

We want to be clear: correlation does not equal causation, and we are not making any claims about ThermaHive improving colony performance. But it is a trend we are actively tracking, and we look forward to sharing more as our data builds.

"The science is there."

Storm, Master Beekeeper and Maryland State Bee Inspector

We also want to share one story we find particularly fascinating. One of our hives was queenless at the time of its first ThermaHive treatment, though queen cells were present. Since that treatment, the hive now has a thriving, mated, and laying queen. That means this queen experienced ThermaHive heat while still inside a queen cell, before she ever had the chance to mate or be born.

We have no way to draw conclusions from one hive and one queen, but we think this will be a valuable long-term data point as we study how ThermaHive affects colony development over time.

Software Update

A Bug, A Fix, and a Big New Feature

We want to be transparent with our community: at the end of May, we identified a bug in the unit's firmware. There were some growing pains as expected with a V1 product, but our engineering team put in the work, tracked down the issue, and got it resolved. We have since been working with our testers to get every unit updated.

And here is the exciting part: with this update comes a major capability upgrade. Units can now receive firmware updates wirelessly, over WiFi. No more connecting to a computer. Future improvements can be pushed out to your unit with ease. This is a meaningful step forward in how we support our tester community.

V2 Development

Smaller, Smarter, Solar-Ready

V2 development is moving along and we have some exciting details to share.

Smaller, sleeker design. We are redesigning the bottom box to fit a standard Langstroth medium super, and will offer both 8-frame and 10-frame options. V2 will be a much more streamlined form factor.
Wireless sensor design is complete. The design is finalized and is ready to move into production for testing ahead of V2's release. The wireless sensor is designed to give you the ability to place multiple sensors throughout the hive for better data coverage, with less hassle.
Solar battery accessory will be compatible with both V1 and V2. No outlet, no problem.
BroodMinder integration is in the works. We are actively developing native BroodMinder connectivity and are targeting V2's release as the milestone for that integration to be ready.
Pre-orders are expected to open in October 2026. You can join the waitlist now to be the first to know.
